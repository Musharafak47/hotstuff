Hotstuff Blockchain Dictionary

DracoBFT
The custom-built consensus engine of the Hotstuff blockchain. Designed for extreme speed with sub-second finality, enabling near-instant trades similar to a centralized exchange.
Order Book State Machine
A core system that processes buy and sell orders at the protocol level, making Hotstuff a trading-first blockchain rather than a simple transfer network.
Programmable Finance Routing Layer
An automated system that routes users to the best available financial service, similar to how Uber matches riders with drivers.
Financial Validators
Validators that act as service providers, connecting the blockchain to real-world systems such as bank transfers and payment applications.
zkTLS (Zero-Knowledge Transport Layer Security)
A privacy technology that verifies bank balances or identities from websites without exposing login credentials or sensitive data.
Change-Log Hash (CLH)
A performance optimization that records only balance changes instead of the full blockchain state, increasing efficiency and speed.
Gasless Trading
A trading model where users do not pay gas fees directly; costs are covered by platforms or third parties for a seamless experience.
Fiat Rails
Infrastructure that allows traditional currencies like USD and EUR to move between banks and the blockchain ecosystem.
On/Off-Ramp
The process of converting fiat money into crypto (on-ramp) or converting crypto back into traditional bank money (off-ramp).
Perpetuals (Perps)
Advanced trading instruments that allow speculation on price movements without owning the underlying asset, often with leverage.
Multi-Venue Vaults
Automated vaults that deploy professional strategies across multiple platforms to generate returns for users.
DeFi (Decentralized Finance)
Financial services such as trading, lending, and borrowing that operate on blockchains without traditional intermediaries.
Layer 1 (L1)
The base blockchain layer where transactions are settled, such as Ethereum or Hotstuff.
Liquidity
The ease with which assets can be bought or sold without significantly impacting price. Hotstuff targets deep liquidity.
Finality
The point at which a transaction becomes irreversible. Sub-second finality means trades are confirmed almost instantly.
